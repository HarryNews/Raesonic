Track Naming Guidelines

Revision Date: Jul 5, 2016
  • Keep the track names close to original, using the symbols available.
    • Use letter case that is most commonly used by the official sources.
      bad: Twenty One Pilots
      good: twenty one pilots
    • Keep the foreign characters, avoid using substitutes.
      bad: Sigur Ros – Saeglopur
      good: Sigur Rós – Sæglópur
    • Try to keep the letter case down in track titles where it's not intended.
      bad: RATATAT – CREAM ON CHROME
      good: RATATAT – Cream on Chrome
  • Include all artists in the artist field, use &+ as the separator.
    • Avoid using feat. or other acronyms when describing multiple artists.
      bad: Darren Korb – In Circles (feat. Ashley Barrett)
      good: Darren Korb &+ Ashley Barrett – In Circles
    • Don't split the artist name with a &+ separator.
      bad: Chase &+ Status
      good: Chase & Status
    • Credit artists that created the track, leave out soundtrack references.
      bad: Valiant Hearts Soundtrack – Main Menu Theme
      good: Daniel Jacob Teper – Little Trinketry
    • Use Various Artists when attributing collections with multiple artists.
      bad: Hotline Miami 2 OST – Full
      good: Various Artists – Hotline Miami 2 Soundtrack
  • Describe track variations in the title field, wrapped in parentheses.
    • Keep the names clean and accurate.
      bad: Deadmau5 – Raise Your Weapon (remixed by madeon)
      good: deadmau5 – Raise Your Weapon (Madeon Remix)
    • Avoid adding meaningless labels to the original track versions.
      bad: Feed Me – Blood Red (Original Mix)
      good: Feed Me – Blood Red
    • Keep cover authors in the artist field, reference original artist in the title.
      bad: Tame Impala – Feels Like We Only Go Backwards (cover by Arctic Monkeys)
      good: Arctic Monkeys – Feels Like We Only Go Backwards (Tame Impala Cover)
    • Use the original track name for live versions.
      bad: Low Roar – I'll Keep Coming (Live on KEXP)
      good: Low Roar – I'll Keep Coming
